Important — safety, conditions & liability

WaveBase is editorial information, not navigational or safety advice. Surf conditions are dynamic and dangerous. Never enter the water based on what you read here. Always verify the day's conditions locally before paddling out.

What our information is — and isn't

WaveBase spot pages describe what a place is generally like — the kind of wave, the typical wind season, the levels it suits. This is editorial content compiled from public sources (Booking, TripAdvisor, Windguru, Beach-Inspector, Open-Meteo, OpenStreetMap and others) and personal experience, labelled per entry under each spot's "Source" line. It is not:

  • A real-time forecast (we don't tell you whether today is safe).
  • Navigation or routing advice (always use a current marine chart for that).
  • A substitute for lifeguard, coastguard or local-knowledge advice.
  • A guarantee that a spot is at a particular level on a particular day — conditions change rapidly with swell, tide, wind and weather.

Before you paddle out

You are responsible for your own safety in the water. Before any session you should:

  • Check today's forecast on a current source (Windguru, Surfline, your local MET service).
  • Read the conditions at the actual spot (size, period, wind, tide, current) and match them to your real ability today.
  • Ask locals or the staff at the nearest surf center about the day's hazards.
  • Respect lifeguard flags, no-swim zones, and any other regulatory signage on the beach.
  • Never surf alone in unfamiliar or advanced spots; tell someone your plan.
