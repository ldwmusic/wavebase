Privacy & cookies

What we collect, and what we don't.

Plain English version. The short story: as little as possible, no cookies for tracking, no selling of personal data to third parties.

The short version

  • You can use SurfGoose without an account. Saved places, trips and compare list then live in your browser only (localStorage) and never reach our servers.
  • If you do create an account, we store your email, a hashed password, optional profile fields you choose to fill in, and your saved spots — so they follow you across devices. We never sell that data.
  • Anonymous traffic stats via Cloudflare Web Analytics — no cookies, no personal profiles. You can switch this off in the cookie banner or via the footer link.
  • No third-party trackers. No Google Analytics, no Facebook Pixel, no remarketing.

What we actually collect

1. Strictly necessary — in your browser only

When you save a spot, build a trip, or add things to your compare list, those choices are stored in your browser via localStorage. They never leave your device. If you clear your browser data, they disappear.

Legal basis: not personal data — it stays on your machine.

2. Anonymous analytics (opt-out)

By default (after you click Accept all) we count anonymous page visits with Cloudflare Web Analytics. This tool was designed to be privacy-friendly: it doesn't set cookies, doesn't fingerprint visitors, and doesn't track individual users across sessions. We only see aggregate numbers like "how many people visited Discover today" or "which spot pages get the most views".

If you reject analytics (in the cookie banner or via "Cookie preferences" in the footer), we don't load the Cloudflare beacon at all from that point on. Your visit becomes invisible to us.

Legal basis: your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR), withdrawable at any time.

3. Email and account data (optional)

You can use SurfGoose without an account. If you choose to create one (via "Login" in the header), here's what we store on our server:

  • Required: your email address and a hashed password (we use bcrypt — we never see your plaintext password and can't recover it).
  • Optional, you can skip these: your name, birth year, home country, sports you do, your surf level. These help us tailor recommendations on the home page but aren't shared with anyone else.
  • Synced from your browser: the spots you save with the heart icon, your trips (including any day notes you type in them), your "Surfed it" log, your reviews and your profile preferences get synced to your account so they follow you across devices. All of it is wiped with your account if you delete it (reviews excepted — see below).
  • When your account was last active, and roughly how long: while you're signed in and have the site open, we store a timestamp of when your account was last on SurfGoose, plus a running total of how long your account has spent actively on the site (the timer only counts while the tab is open and in focus, and treats a gap of more than 5 minutes as a new visit). It tells us which accounts are still in use and how much the service gets used — it is not a record of which individual pages you visited (that's the separate, consent-gated analytics described below). Legal basis: our legitimate interest in running, sizing and securing the service (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

Legal basis: contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) for the data needed to provide the account, and your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) for optional profile fields. You can delete your account any time by emailing info.surfgoose@gmail.com or by using the “Delete account” button at the bottom of your account page. We wipe the account record, your saved spots, your trips and your surf log immediately on request.

Reviews stay live. Reviews you publish (on a spot, center or stay page) are public content other surfers rely on for honest fine print — we don’t want a single account deletion to wipe out social proof for everyone else. When you delete your account, your reviews stay visible with the display name you typed when posting (you knew it was public; we don’t change what you wrote). The link to your account is severed, so no-one can edit those reviews any more. If you want a specific review removed too, delete or edit it from the spot page or your account-page “My reviews” block before you delete your account.

The two editors of SurfGoose (Lode De Waegenaere and Michiel De Cooman) can view account data and activity in an internal admin tool for product improvement and customer support. The admin tool never exposes plaintext passwords (we don’t store them — only a bcrypt hash) and is gated by an email allowlist; no other party has access.

4. Product analytics (opt-in)

If you click Accept all in the cookie banner, we record a handful of anonymous events — on our own server, never sent to any third party — to understand what's working: which pages get visited, which "Book now" buttons get clicked, how the signup flow performs, and what people search for. Each event carries a per-browser-session ID (a random string in sessionStorage that resets when you close the tab) and, if you're signed in, your user account ID. Never an IP address, never a fingerprint, never sold or shared.

If you reject analytics, none of these session-linked events are sent — same as the Cloudflare aggregate above.

Legal basis: your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR), withdrawable at any time via the cookie banner.

One exception — a few anonymous counters. Even if you don't accept analytics cookies, a small number of anonymous tallies still run. None of them carry a session ID, an account ID, an IP address, or anything that points back to you — they're the equivalent of tally marks or lines in a server log, and we never combine them with any other data. They are:

  • A page-hit count. When you open a spot, center or stay page we add +1 to an anonymous counter for that page (so we can see, for example, that "Tenda Beach was viewed 40 times"). Stores only which page and when.
  • A time-on-page average. When you leave such a page we record how many seconds it was actively open (the timer pauses when the tab is hidden, is capped at 30 minutes, and ignores visits under 2 seconds), so we can see roughly how long people read each page. Stores only the page and a number of seconds — never a trail of where you went.
  • A cookie-choice tally. When the cookie banner is shown and when you click Accept or Reject, we add +1 to the matching counter, so we know what share of visitors accept analytics. We can't gate the "reject" tally behind consent, so it runs here. It records only the choice, not who made it.
  • A visit tally. The first page you open in a browser session adds +1 to a site-wide visit counter (so we can see, for example, "1,200 visits in the last 30 days"). Stores only the moment it happened — nothing about you or what you looked at.
  • A time-on-site average. When you leave the site we record how many seconds your visit was actively on screen in total (same honest timer as above: pauses when the tab is hidden; visits under 10 seconds are never sent at all). So that two halves of one visit aren't counted as two visits, this number travels with a random label that exists only for the duration of your browser session, is never stored with your account or any page trail, and can't be used to recognise you later.

Because none of these can identify a person, they run on our legitimate interest in understanding which content is useful and how our consent banner performs (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) rather than on consent.

5. Affiliate links (no data shared by us)

Some "Book now" buttons on the site link to partners like Booking.com or stay-property websites. When you click such a link, the partner's site sets its own cookies and processes data according to its own privacy policy. We earn a small commission if you book through them; we never share your personal data with them beyond what you actively send by clicking the link and continuing on their site.

6. Server logs

Our hosting provider (Cloudflare) keeps standard server logs (IP address, requested URL, timestamp) for security and infrastructure purposes, typically for a short period (days, not months). We don't access these logs unless investigating an incident.

What we don't do

  • No advertising cookies
  • No retargeting / behavioural advertising
  • No selling of personal data to brokers
  • No tracking pixels from social networks
  • No Google Fonts loaded from Google's CDN (we self-host the font files so your IP isn't sent to Google)

Your rights under GDPR

Even though we collect very little, EU law gives you the following rights for any personal data we do process. You can exercise them by emailing info.surfgoose@gmail.com:

  • Right of access — ask what we have on you
  • Right to rectification — correct mistakes
  • Right to erasure — ask us to delete your data
  • Right to object — ask us to stop processing
  • Right to data portability — get your data in a portable format
  • Right to lodge a complaint with the Belgian Data Protection Authority (GBA) if you think we're handling your data badly

How long we keep things

  • localStorage data (saved places, trips, profile when not signed in): as long as you keep it — you control it from your browser settings.
  • Account data (email, hashed password, optional profile fields, saved spots, trips, surf log, profile preferences): kept for as long as your account exists. Deleted within 30 days of you asking us to delete your account (reviews stay live as described above).
  • Cloudflare Web Analytics: Cloudflare retains anonymous aggregate metrics for about 6 months by default.
  • Server logs: short-term (days to a few weeks) per Cloudflare's and Render's standard retention.

Who else sees your data (processors)

We use the following service providers ("processors") to run the site:

  • Cloudflare, Inc. (USA, with EU data centres) — hosting of the frontend pages and anonymous analytics. Cloudflare is bound by Standard Contractual Clauses for EU data transfers and is certified under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework.
  • Render Services, Inc. (USA) — hosting of our API server (the part that handles your account, login and saved spots). Render is bound by Standard Contractual Clauses for EU data transfers. Used only if you create an account.
  • MongoDB, Inc. / MongoDB Atlas (managed cloud database) — storage for account records and saved-spots. Bound by Standard Contractual Clauses for EU data transfers. Used only if you create an account.
  • GitHub — we store the site's source code (no visitor data).

If SurfGoose changes hands

If SurfGoose is ever sold, merged with another organisation, or transferred to a successor, the data you've trusted us with may transfer to the new owner. We'll let you know in advance via this page (and where possible by email to account holders) so you can object or delete your data first. The new owner must continue to honour this privacy policy or notify you of changes.
